Life on Tenerife is easy and sunny — but it stays unpredictable for everyone. A slip on wet tiles, an awkward landing while surfing, a misstep on a mountain trail. These things don't only happen to "someone else"; they can happen to any of us. The good news is that you can be ready for the unexpected in advance — calmly, without anxiety.

## Why prepare for an accident in particular

Illness often gives you warning signs. An accident doesn't. It happens in a second and changes your plans for weeks.

- an injury during travel or sport;
- a fall at home or in the street;
- a small everyday mishap that suddenly knocks you off balance.

When this happens far from home, the pain comes with a second question: "how am I going to handle all of this now?" That second weight — the financial and emotional one — is exactly what a policy lifts off your shoulders.

## What an accident policy gives you

It isn't about treatment — it's about support while you get back on your feet. Cover usually includes:

- a cash benefit for an injury or fracture;
- support during recovery, when you can't work as you normally would;
- payouts for loss of ability to work;
- help for your family in the hardest scenarios.

The exact mix depends on the policy you choose, but the idea is the same: you have a financial cushion precisely when you need it most.

> Peace of mind isn't when nothing happens. It's knowing you'll be fine if it does.

## How it changes the way life feels

Readiness works quietly. You still hike the hills, ride your bike, play with the kids on the beach — but without the background "what if." The nagging worry that something might go wrong stops standing over your shoulder, because you already have an answer.

That matters even more as an expat: you don't have the familiar safety net of family and old connections nearby, so your own preparedness becomes the steady ground under your feet.

## Who this resonates with most

- active people — running, surfing, cycling, the mountains;
- parents who want to know their family is protected;
- the self-employed, for whom a pause in work is keenly felt;
- anyone who simply wants to live with more ease.

You don't have to be a thrill-seeker. It's enough to be someone who values a little certainty about tomorrow.
