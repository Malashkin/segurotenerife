When you move to Tenerife, one of the first questions is how healthcare works here and where to go if something hurts. Here's how private healthcare works for an expat.

## How to see a doctor

With a private policy the process is simple:

- choose a clinic or doctor from the network your policy gives access to;
- book (by phone, app or website);
- attend your appointment — without the long queues typical of the public system.

You can usually see a primary-care doctor or specialist within a few days.

## Language of the appointment

Tenerife has plenty of **English-speaking doctors**, and in areas popular with expats you'll also find other languages. This removes newcomers' biggest worry: you can get treated without speaking Spanish.

## Private or public

- **Public** — free for residents who pay social contributions, but waits for specialists can run into weeks or months.
- **Private** — a paid policy, but fast access to specialists, diagnostics and often a more convenient service.

Many people combine both: the public system as a base, private cover for speed.

## What a private policy covers

Typically: a primary-care doctor and dozens of specialties, 24/7 emergencies, tests and diagnostics, hospitalisation, basic dental. The exact set depends on the policy you choose.

> If you're applying for residency, a private policy with no co-payments also satisfies the consulate requirement — two in one.
