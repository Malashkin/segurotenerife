When choosing medical insurance in Spain you'll meet two types of policy: **with co-payments** (con copago) and **without** (sin copago). Here's the difference in plain words.

## Policy with co-payments (con copago)

- Lower monthly premium.
- You pay a small fixed fee for each visit or procedure.
- Suits people who rarely see a doctor.

The downside: with active treatment the fees pile up and the saving disappears. And crucially — **it doesn't work for a visa**.

## Policy without co-payments (sin copago)

- Slightly higher monthly premium.
- No per-visit fees — everything is included.
- This is the version consulates require for a visa and residency.

## Which to choose

- **Applying for residency or a visa** — no co-payments only. There's no alternative.
- **Already a resident and using healthcare a lot** (family, children, chronic conditions) — no co-payments is more convenient and predictable.
- **You barely see doctors and don't need a visa** — a co-payment policy can be considered.

> For most newcomers on Tenerife the answer is the same: a no-co-payment policy — it works for the visa and keeps costs transparent.
