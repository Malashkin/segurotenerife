When you move with your family, insurance matters even more: you need cover for adults and children alike. Here's what to look for.

## One policy for the whole family

A family is usually covered by **a single policy**: it's easier to manage and often cheaper than separate policies for each person. An adult can be added later too.

## Children: paediatrician and vaccines

A family medical policy includes a **paediatrician** and specialist appointments. The set of vaccinations depends on the specific policy — worth checking in advance, especially if your child is starting nursery or school.

## Pregnancy and childbirth

Pregnancy care and childbirth are covered, but they almost always have a **waiting period (carencia)** — a few months from the start date. So if you're planning a baby, take the policy out early.

## What else to look at

- **No co-payments** — if a family member is also applying for residency.
- **Clinics nearby** — convenient when you have children.
- **Doctors' languages** — Tenerife has English-speaking paediatricians.

> Tip: take the policy out early — because of waiting periods, some services (childbirth, certain procedures) may not be available immediately on day one.
