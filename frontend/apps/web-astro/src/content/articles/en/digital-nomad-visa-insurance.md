The Digital Nomad Visa (DNV) lets you live in Spain legally while working remotely for companies based outside the country. Among the required documents is **medical insurance**, held to the same strict standards as other residency visas.

## What the DNV policy must look like

- **Full coverage with no co-payments** — paying out of pocket for visits is not allowed.
- **No waiting periods** — coverage from day one.
- **Valid across all of Spain**, including Tenerife and the Canaries.
- **With a certificate** for the consulate / immigration office confirming compliance.

So a cheap "annual" travel policy with an excess won't do — it's almost always rejected.

## Common mistakes when applying for the DNV

- Buying a cheap travel policy and getting refused on the medical part.
- Choosing a policy with co-payments, not knowing the visa needs one without them.
- Leaving it late — the certificate has to be ready before you apply.

## Worth knowing

A DNV policy isn't just "a document for the visa". It genuinely covers doctors, emergencies, diagnostics and hospitalisation across Spain — which matters when you're relocating for the long term and want to be treated without queues.

> If you're moving with a partner or children on the DNV, they can often be added to a single family policy.
