If you're starting out in Spain as an **autónomo** (self-employed) or running a small company, medical insurance comes up from two sides: for your own residency and for your team.

## For yourself: residency and access to care

When obtaining residency through an entrepreneur or business visa, you need a **no-co-payment medical policy with a certificate** — the same requirements as other visas. And for an already-working autónomo, private insurance is simply convenient: fast access to doctors without queues, which matters when time is money.

## For the team: group policies

Employees can be covered by a **corporate (group) policy**. It is:

- an attractive perk for the team;
- single management instead of separate policies;
- terms that depend on the number of employees.

## What to consider

- **Visa type** — it determines the policy requirements.
- **How many people you're insuring** — this drives the group-policy cost.
- **Tax details** — whether the insurance counts as an expense is best checked with your gestor; we help with choosing the policy itself.

> For an autónomo applying for residency, the same rule applies: the policy must have no co-payments and come with a certificate.
